## Short Description (manifest — 132 char max)
Route tab audio to any connected output device — switch between speakers, headphones, Bluetooth, and HDMI with one click.

## Full Description (paste into Partner Center)

Audio Redirector gives you full control over where each browser tab sends its sound. Connected multiple audio devices — laptop speakers, wired headphones, a Bluetooth speaker, or an HDMI monitor? Now you can pick exactly which device plays the audio for every individual tab, all with a single click from the toolbar.

Open YouTube in one tab routed to your headphones, keep a video call on your laptop speakers in another, and send a music stream to your Bluetooth speaker — all at the same time. Audio Redirector remembers your choice while the tab is open, and automatically re-applies it if the page reloads.

Key features:
• Per-tab audio routing — assign a different output device to each tab independently.
• One-click switching — open the popup, pick a device, and audio redirects instantly.
• Auto-detection — all connected audio output devices (speakers, headphones, Bluetooth, USB, HDMI) are listed automatically.
• Persistent selection — your device choice sticks for the tab's lifetime, even across page navigations.
• Lightweight and private — zero data collection, no analytics, no network requests. Everything stays in your browser.
• Works everywhere — YouTube, Spotify, Netflix, SoundCloud, Twitch, and any website that plays audio or video.

How it works:
1. Navigate to any website that plays audio (e.g., youtube.com).
2. Click the Audio Redirector icon in the toolbar.
3. Select your preferred output device from the list.
4. Audio switches immediately — no page reload needed.

Note: On first use, the browser may ask for microphone permission on the page. This is a browser requirement to reveal audio device names — Audio Redirector does NOT record or access your microphone.

Built with Manifest V3. No third-party libraries. Completely open source.
